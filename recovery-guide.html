Wire Fraud Recovery Guide

A comprehensive step-by-step guide to recover from wire fraud and minimize losses. Time is critical—follow these steps immediately.

URGENT: If you suspect wire fraud, take action immediately. Every minute counts. Recovery rates drop dramatically after 24 hours.

Verify the Fraud Immediately

  • Contact the sender directly via phone (not email) to confirm they authorized the transfer
  • Check your internal approval records and wire authorization logs
  • Review the receiving bank details—do they match your vendor's legitimate account?
  • Look for subtle variations in email addresses or domain names

Timeline: Must complete within 30 minutes of discovery

Halt Further Transactions

  • Immediately contact your sending bank's fraud department
  • Request an emergency hold on the account to prevent additional unauthorized transfers
  • Obtain a direct contact number and case reference number
  • Ask about "recall" options for the specific wire transfer

Timeline: Must happen within 2 hours for maximum effectiveness

File Official Reports

  • Report to the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) at ic3.gov
  • File a report with your local law enforcement agency
  • File a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at reportfraud.ftc.gov
  • Document all evidence: emails, timestamps, wire instructions, account details

Info Needed: Wire reference numbers, amounts, dates, sender details, receiving bank information

Contact Receiving Bank

  • Call the receiving bank immediately with fraud alert
  • Request immediate freeze of the beneficiary account
  • Provide wire reference number, amount, and exact timestamp
  • Ask for written confirmation of the account freeze
  • Request information about the account holder for law enforcement

Success Rate: 78% if requested within 24 hours of transfer

Engage Legal Representation

  • Hire an attorney specializing in fraud recovery
  • Request emergency court orders to freeze beneficiary accounts
  • Initiate asset preservation actions before funds are withdrawn
  • Prepare for potential civil litigation against the receiving bank

Critical: Professional legal action significantly increases recovery probability

Coordinate Multi-Channel Response

Simultaneously pursue recovery through multiple channels for maximum effectiveness:

  • Banking: Direct contact with both sending and receiving institutions
  • Law Enforcement: FBI, local police, financial crimes units
  • International: INTERPOL for cross-border fund tracking
  • Compliance: FinCEN reporting for suspicious activity patterns

Prevention Framework

Implement these controls to prevent future wire fraud incidents:

  • Dual Verification: Require phone callback confirmation for all wire transfers
  • Vendor Whitelisting: Maintain approved list of payee accounts
  • Amount Thresholds: Escalate review for transfers above specified limits
  • Email Authentication: Deploy DMARC, SPF, and DKIM protocols
  • Employee Training: Monthly fraud awareness and verification procedures
  • Account Monitoring: Real-time alerts for unusual wire activity patterns
